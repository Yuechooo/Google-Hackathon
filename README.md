# A Film's Life in NYC
**Helping niche films find their audience through data-driven storytelling**

---

## Overview

Independent filmmakers don't lack talent — they lack infrastructure. *A Film's Life in NYC* is a platform that gives producers the same audience intelligence that studios take for granted: who's out there, where they are, and how to reach them.

We connect film metadata with NYC open datasets to surface hyper-local audience insights, generate actionable Go-To-Market strategies, and present everything through a clean, interactive dashboard.

---

## The Problem

Most niche films are distributed broadly and marketed generically — because the tools to do otherwise haven't existed. Filmmakers are left guessing at their audience, burning budget on unfocused campaigns, and watching genuinely good work go unseen.

The data to do this better already exists. It just hasn't been connected to the right problem.

---

## What We Built

A platform that takes a film's core attributes — genre, themes, tone, subject matter — and maps them to real audience segments using NYC neighborhood demographics, cultural event data, and behavioral patterns.

From there, it generates a tailored GTM strategy: where to screen, who to partner with, when to release, and how to position. Everything surfaces in a dashboard built for filmmakers, not data scientists.

### Core Features

**Audience Matching Engine**
Segments NYC audiences by neighborhood, demographic, and interest profile. Matches film attributes to the communities most likely to respond — before a dollar of marketing is spent.

**GTM Strategy Builder**
Produces a concrete, location-specific marketing plan. Venue recommendations, community organization targets, press contacts, and release timing — all grounded in data.

**Interactive Dashboard**
Geographic heatmaps, audience size estimates, engagement likelihood scores, and campaign timelines. Built to be readable at a glance and actionable immediately.

**MCP + NYC Open Data Integration**
Uses Modular Control Pipelines to query and process NYC open datasets in real time — demographics, cultural organizations, venue directories, foot traffic patterns, and more.

## Why NYC

New York is one of the most demographically complex cities in the world — which makes it an ideal environment to build and test audience-matching logic. The borough-level variation in language, culture, income, and interest is granular enough to stress-test the model and meaningful enough to produce real signal.

The architecture is designed to extend to other cities as datasets become available.
